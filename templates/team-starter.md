# Team Collaboration Constitution: placeholder

A team variant of the Collaboration Constitution is in development within Doug's immediate research team.

## Expected contents (provisional)

- **Shared core:** the values all team members commit to jointly. Likely overlaps substantially with the personal constitution's commitments to open and usable work, humanistic practice, and critical thinking and judgement.
- **Operational covenant:** shared workflow defaults, consent boundaries, provenance expectations, documentation practices, and reproducibility norms.
- **Practices and examples:** onboarding material, boundary examples, interpretation, case patterns, and examples of appropriate and inappropriate automation.
- **Personal variations:** explicit allowance for each team member to maintain their own personal constitution that extends (but does not contradict) the shared core. The team version sets the floor; personal versions set the ceiling.
- **Coordination norms:** team-specific working agreements that do not fit in a personal document: peer review practice before public commits, shared credit policies, incident-response patterns for AI-related issues, a collective amendment process, and how new team members are onboarded into the values.

## Why this is a placeholder

The team variant should emerge from real team practice, not be written in advance of it. Doug's team is in the process of adopting the personal constitution individually; the shared patterns and friction points from that adoption will inform the team version.

## Starting point

Until the team version is written, the [personal-starter template](personal-starter.md) is the best starting point for individual team members who want to draft their own. A team constitution is expected to layer on top: each team member keeps their own personal constitution; the team document records the shared core, operational covenant, practices, and coordination norms.
