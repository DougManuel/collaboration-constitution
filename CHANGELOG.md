# Changelog

All notable changes to the Collaboration Constitution are recorded here.

This document follows the [Keep a Changelog](https://keepachangelog.com/en/1.1.0/) format. Versioning is loose semantic:

- **Major** (1.0, 2.0): structural or values-level changes; principles added, removed, or reshaped.
- **Minor** (0.1, 0.2): new operational guidance; clarifications that change how the constitution is applied.
- **Patch** (0.1.1, 0.1.2): typographical and editorial edits; no change in meaning.

## [Unreleased], working draft toward 0.2.0

### Changed

- Repository made public for comment (2026-06-12).
- Reworked the constitution into a shorter preliminary constitutional core.
- Replaced the four-chapter structure with three durable commitments:
  1. Humanistic
  2. Open and usable
  3. Critical thinking and judgement
- Reframed "Quality" as scientific integrity, emphasizing uncertainty, evidence, provenance, disagreement, and limitations.
- Reframed the local-first principle around avoiding unnecessary dependence on inaccessible infrastructure rather than around a fixed frontier/local model distinction.
- Broadened the documentation principle into an open, usable, and equitable work commitment for collaborators across science domains, trainees, and practitioners.
- Clarified that AI should strengthen human understanding, creativity, insight, and judgement.
- Added explicit language on assumptions, bias, institutional influences, and automation bias.
- Updated authorship language to reflect drafting dialogue with AI coding agents.
- Updated the personal starter template to match the layered structure.

### Added

- `docs/operational-covenant.md` for workflow defaults, consent boundaries, openness, provenance, testing, communication, AI workflow, memory, and git/publication practices.
- `docs/practices-and-examples.md` for commentary, examples, uncertainty language, automation patterns, bias checks, and memory practices.

### Preserved

- The core commitments to equity in global health, open and usable work, human accountability, human flourishing, consent before consequential action, and knowing when not to use AI.

## [0.1.0], 2026-04-22 (alpha)

### Added

- Preamble stating the equity-in-global-health orientation.
- Four chapters: Mission, Quality, Augmentation contract, Epistemic practice.
- Ten principles:
  1. Equity before convenience
  2. Open science as infrastructure
  3. The local path must always exist
  4. Methodological defensibility
  5. Pedagogy is part of the work
  6. AI augments; it does not replace
  7. Epistemic honesty
  8. Consent before consequential actions
  9. Know when not to use AI
  10. The boundary between AI and human judgement will shift
- "How this applies": six operational rules drawn from the principles.
- Acknowledgements naming Anthropic's Claude Constitution as inspiration and the different axis this document operates on.
- Authorship statement.
- CC-BY-4.0 licence with citation guidance.
- Personal-starter template for forks.
- Team-starter placeholder (to be filled as Doug's immediate research team adopts a team variant).
- Amendment issue template.

### Status

**Alpha.** Repository is private during the alpha period. Being shared with Doug's immediate research team for feedback. No Zenodo DOI minted yet; DOI integration pending the transition from private to public.

### Known open items

- A `docs/why.md` essay explains the reasoning but may expand as feedback arrives.
- The principle ordering, the tension between principles 4 (defensibility) and 6 (augmentation), and the specific LMIC framing are all expected to attract comment during alpha.
